REFEREE REPORT(S):
Referee: Preliminary reviewer

COMMENTS TO THE AUTHOR(S)
This paper discusses the role of dimensional analysis in general relativity. Many text (textbooks, research papers) use abstract differential geometry to describe physical effects related to general relativity theory. In this approach one does not pay too much attention to physical interpretation of formulas. In particular, the dimensions of the considered quantities are not discussed, the argument being that all  coordinates are equally good. Thus, e.g. some spatial coordinate can have dimension of length, some other - different dimension. It is not very clear what are dimensions of more complicated quantities, like e.g. the Einstein tensor, and therefore what is their physical meaning. The present paper elucidates this problem.
In my opinion the paper should be refereed.

Referee: 2

COMMENTS TO THE AUTHOR(S)
A delightful and very useful paper.  While the material is  not novel, it addresses  an area of  great and heated confusion  in the literature. One only has to read the talk pages on Wikipedia's GR articles to see how people get worked up over the question of whether all components of the tensor have to have the same dimensions or not to see this.  

The paper reads as a bit of a polemic, perhaps not surprisingly given that a key reference is by Clifford Truesdell, himself an entertaining and usually correct polemicist, but this style adds to the appeal of the text.  

I rated the the level of the text as "graduate," but this paper should, and probably will, be widely read and cited by specialists as well as by students.

I strongly recommend that we accept in its  present form.

Referee: 3
